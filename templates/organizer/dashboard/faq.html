FAQs

Answers to common questions about managing events on EventHub

How do I access the organizer portal?

Go to /organizer/login/ and sign in with your organizer account. After login you are redirected to the Dashboard. Only users with the organizer role (or superuser access) can use this portal.

What sections are available in the organizer portal?

The top navigation includes:

  • Dashboard — overview metrics and quick actions
  • Events — create and manage events
  • Attendees — customer directory
  • Bookings — ticket sales ledger
  • Promotions — discount codes
  • Reviews — attendee feedback
  • Settings — profile, payments, team, API keys
  • FAQs — this help page

Ticket check-in is available at /organizer/tickets/ (not shown in the main nav).

Why was I redirected to the login page?

You are redirected when your session has expired or you are not signed in as an organizer. Log in again at /organizer/login/. If the problem persists, clear your browser cache or try a private window.

What is the difference between organizer and attendee accounts?

Attendee accounts browse events, book tickets, and manage personal bookings. Organizer accounts create events, configure payments, approve sales, check in attendees, and track performance on the Dashboard. You need an organizer role to access this portal.

What do the dashboard metric cards show?

The top row displays:

  • Total Events — number of events you have created
  • Tickets Sold — total valid ticket quantities across events
  • Total Revenue — gross sales in KES from valid tickets
  • Attendees — unique buyers (based on ticket records)

Percentage change badges appear when the API provides comparison data.

How does the Revenue Overview chart work?

The line chart shows gross revenue over time. Use the period selector to switch between Last 7 days, 4 weeks, 12 months (default), and Year to date. Amounts are shown in Kenyan Shillings (KES).

What are Pending Payment Approvals?

When an attendee pays via M-Pesa and the payment requires manual review, the order appears here. You can:

  • View the payment screenshot submitted by the attendee
  • Approve — issues the ticket to the buyer
  • Reject — declines the order

Auto-verified payments are issued without your action.

What is the Performance Score?

The Performance Score (0–100) summarizes how well your events are doing. It considers factors such as average rating and ticket fulfillment rate. Use it as a quick health check alongside your Dashboard revenue chart and Bookings ledger.

How do notifications work on the dashboard?

The notifications panel lists recent alerts (new bookings, payment reviews, event updates, etc.). Click an unread notification to mark it read, or use Mark all read to clear the unread state.

How do I create a new event?

Go to Events and click Create Event (or use the Quick Action on the Dashboard). Fill in the title, category, start/end date and time, venue, capacity, and base price. Optionally add VIP/VVIP tiers and a banner image, then save.

Why is my event status "Pending"?

New events are submitted for admin approval before they can go live. Until approved, the status stays pending even if you attempt to publish. You will be notified when an admin approves or rejects your event.

What event categories are available?

When creating an event you can choose from: Music, Technology, Business, Sports, Arts, and Food. Pick the category that best matches your event for discoverability.

How do VIP and VVIP ticket tiers work?

Beyond the base ticket price, you can enable optional VIP and VVIP tiers with separate prices. Check the tier checkbox in the event form and set each price. Attendees choose their tier at checkout.

What are the banner image requirements?

Banner images must be PNG, JPEG, SVG, WEBP, or GIF and no larger than 5 MB. A compelling banner improves visibility on the public event listing.

Can I edit an event after it is published?

Yes. Click the event card on the Events page to open the edit modal. Changes to key details may trigger a notification email. Significant changes to a live event should be communicated to existing ticket holders.

How do I see tickets sold for a specific event?

Each event card shows tickets sold / capacity. For deeper detail, filter Bookings and Attendees by that event, or review the Revenue Overview chart on the Dashboard.

How do attendees pay for tickets?

Attendees pay via M-Pesa at checkout. They receive an STK Push on their phone, enter their PIN, and receive instant confirmation. Some payments may require your manual approval if flagged for review.

How do I set up my M-Pesa payment details?

Go to Settings → Payment. Enter your display name (required) and at least one of:

  • Paybill number
  • Till number
  • Pochi la Biashara
  • Send Money number

Save your settings before attendees can pay you directly.

What is bank settlement and do I need it?

Bank settlement is optional. It lets EventHub transfer your earnings to a bank account. Provide your bank name, account number, account holder name, and routing/SWIFT code under Settings → Payment. M-Pesa details are still required for attendee checkout.

When do I receive my payouts?

Payouts are typically processed within 3–5 business days after your event concludes and all pending orders are resolved. Ensure your bank or M-Pesa settlement details are up to date in Settings.

How do I approve a manual M-Pesa payment?

On the Dashboard, find the order under Pending Payment Approvals. Review the attendee details and payment screenshot, then click Approve to issue tickets or Reject if the payment is invalid.

What happens if I reject a payment?

Rejecting an order cancels the booking attempt. No ticket is issued. The attendee may need to retry checkout or contact you if they believe the payment was valid.

Where can I see all ticket sales?

Open Bookings from the navigation bar. This ledger lists every ticket purchase with customer name, email, event, amount, purchase date, and status.

How do I search for a specific booking?

Use the search box to find bookings by customer name, email, or ticket number. Combine with the event and status filters to narrow results.

What do booking statuses mean?
  • Valid — ticket is active and can be used for entry
  • Checked In — attendee has been scanned or manually checked in
  • Cancelled — ticket is no longer valid
How do I view full booking details?

Click View on any booking row. The detail modal shows the ticket QR payload, event name, customer info, quantity, total paid, purchase date, status, and check-in timestamp (if applicable).

Can I export bookings?

Export options are available from the Bookings and Check-in pages depending on your configuration. Use export to download attendee lists for offline use or external reporting.

How do I access the check-in scanner?

Navigate to /organizer/tickets/. This page is dedicated to ticket verification and check-in. It is not linked in the main navigation bar, so bookmark it for event day.

How do I scan attendee QR codes?

On the Tickets page, click Start Scanner and allow camera access when prompted. Point the camera at the attendee's QR code. A valid ticket is verified and checked in automatically.

Can I check in attendees manually without a QR code?

Yes. Enter the ticket number in the Quick Check-in field and click Verify. If the ticket is valid, it will be checked in. You can also use the Check In button on individual rows in the ticket table.

What if a ticket is already checked in?

The system will show an error indicating the ticket has already been used. Each ticket can only be checked in once. If you believe this is a mistake, search the booking in Bookings to confirm the check-in timestamp.

Why won't the camera scanner work?

Common fixes:

  • Grant camera permission in your browser (Safari: Settings → Safari → Camera)
  • Use HTTPS — camera access requires a secure connection
  • Try Chrome or Firefox if Safari blocks the camera
  • Ensure adequate lighting and hold the QR code steady

As a fallback, use manual ticket number entry.

What do the check-in stats show?

The Tickets page displays Total Tickets, Checked In, Check-in Rate %, and Recent Check-ins (last 24 hours). Filter by event to see stats for a single event.

What is the Attendees page?

The Attendees directory aggregates everyone who has purchased tickets from you. It shows name, email, phone, events booked, total tickets, lifetime value (KES), and last activity — useful for CRM and follow-up.

How is "Total Value" calculated for an attendee?

Total Value is the sum of all valid ticket purchases by that customer (matched by email) across your events, displayed in Kenyan Shillings.

Can I filter attendees by event?

Yes. Use the event dropdown filter to show only customers who purchased tickets for a specific event. Combine with the search box to find individuals by name, email, or phone.

How is revenue calculated on the Dashboard?

Revenue is the gross total from valid ticket sales across your events, in KES. Cancelled or refunded tickets are excluded. The Revenue Overview chart lets you view totals over 7 days, 4 weeks, 12 months, or year to date.

What is the difference between tickets sold and attendance?

Tickets sold is the number of valid tickets purchased. Attendance (or checked-in count) is how many of those tickets were actually scanned or manually checked in at the venue. Check-in stats are available on the Tickets page.

How do I create a promo code?

Go to Promotions and click Create Promo. Enter a code (auto-uppercased), choose percentage or fixed KES discount, set the value, optional event scope, expiry date, and usage limit.

Can a promo code apply to only one event?

Yes. When creating or editing a promo, select a specific event from the dropdown. Leave the event field blank to apply the discount to all your events.

How do usage limits work?

Set a maximum number of redemptions when creating the promo. The table shows used / limit. Once the limit is reached, the code can no longer be applied at checkout.

How do I deactivate a promotion?

Open the promo in the Promotions table and toggle Deactivate. Deactivated codes are rejected at checkout. You can reactivate them later or delete the promo entirely.

How do I respond to an attendee review?

Go to Reviews, find the review, and click Respond. Write your reply in the modal and submit. Your response appears publicly alongside the original review.

Can I filter reviews by event?

Yes. Use the event filter dropdown at the top of the Reviews page to show feedback for a single event. Summary cards update to reflect the filtered set.

What do the review summary cards show?

The summary shows Average Rating (star average), Total Reviews, and Response Rate — the percentage of reviews you have replied to.

How do I update my organization profile?

Go to Settings → General. Update your organization name, email, phone, website, and bio, then click Save. This information may appear on your public organizer profile and event pages.

How do I invite a team member?

Go to Settings → Team and click Add Member. Enter their email and assign a role:

  • Viewer — read-only access
  • Editor — can manage events and content
  • Admin — full access including settings
How do I remove a team member?

On the Team tab, click Remove next to the member and confirm. They will immediately lose access to your organizer account.

How do I generate an API key?

Go to Settings → API Keys and click Generate Key. Give it a descriptive name. The secret key (prefixed eh_live_) is shown once — copy and store it securely. Use it to integrate with the EventHub API.

What happens if I revoke an API key?

Revoked keys stop working immediately. Any integration using that key will receive authentication errors. Generate a new key and update your integration to restore access.

Why does my data look like sample/demo content?

Some features (promotions, reviews, support tickets) may display sample data when the backend API is unavailable and the app falls back to local storage mocks. Live bookings, events, check-in, and payment approvals use real data when the API is connected.

I get a 401 error or blank page — what should I do?

Your auth token may have expired. Log out and sign in again at /organizer/login/. If using multiple tabs, refresh after logging in. Clear organizer_access_token from local storage only as a last resort.

My event won't publish — what can I check?
  • Confirm the event status — new events need admin approval
  • Ensure required fields (title, date, venue, capacity, price) are filled
  • Verify M-Pesa payment settings are configured in Settings
  • Check notifications for approval or rejection messages
Attendee says they paid but has no ticket — what do I do?

Check Pending Payment Approvals on the Dashboard for orders awaiting review. Search their email in Bookings. If payment was auto-verified, the ticket should appear with status valid. Approve manual-review orders to issue the ticket.
